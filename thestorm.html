The Storms

As the time flows, in August, the time there was PASCERVEL report, I was playing with my phone before class and then the teacher arrived and everyone's phone was collected because of me. I was so ashamed and I apologized to my teacher. And then I started to read the Bible more and more, and I was able to read from Matthew to Romans. And then I also started to be more responsible with my phone and not playing games but reading the Bible instead. And there was also a problem in school that I have to face, which is the grading system. The more you make a mistake, the more you lose points, and I was so afraid of making mistakes because I don't want to lose points. But then I realized that making mistakes is part of learning and growing, and I shouldn't be afraid of it. I also struggled with time management and group work, but I learned to manage my time better and be more active in. Because of these stroms, I have learned that God gave us a challenges to make us stronger and to help us grow, and led me to read His Words more and more. And another type of storm happened, in our church where me and my church-mate (let’s call her Mixed Number) got a misunderstanding about our communication because of how I answered because she’s not answering on me which broke our friendship. I thought it was a righteous anger but, knowingly, I realized it was a wrath. We apologized to each other but it’s only on chat and not to reconcile which hurts me because of how far we’ve been close before. As the day flows, in school time, I was depressed, can’t answer the quiz properly, and I always cried out when I thinked of her. My classmates had noticed this kind of personality I had, and when they asked “Are you okay?” And I didn’t answer. I open up where I told these stories of what happened. And they give me tips like “Apologize again face to face” “just wait for it” and “Remember, Isaiah 60:22, Psalm 34:18, The Lord will help you bro”. This is where I gained confidence but on that, I’m still too shy and did not talk to each other. One day, my close friend (also my church-mate) did isolated me, because of what I’ve done to her. Same thing happened as I sorrow for Mixed Number, in school where I was even more depressed. I opened up again and told it to my classmates even my crush listened on it and gave me an advice. Despite of this depression age, I decided to not think about them and focused on studying more. And I found out today she will probably leave here. I’m still sad but I know that God will help me to get through this and I will be okay. I just need to focus on my studies and not think about her anymore. I will pray for her and for myself that God will heal our hearts and that we can reconcile one day. I know that God has a plan for me and that He will use this experience to make me stronger and to help me grow. I will trust in Him and His timing, and I will keep moving forward with faith and hope.
